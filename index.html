FAQ

Local Business Questions
Do you work with businesses outside of Elsberry / Lincoln County?▾
Yes — I serve businesses across the greater Missouri area. Remote-friendly work like websites, software setup, and automation can be done from anywhere. For hands-on PC repair or networking, I work within a reasonable drive of Elsberry.
I'm not tech-savvy at all. Is that a problem?▾
That's exactly who I built this for. I'll explain everything in plain language, train you on what you need to use, and make sure you're comfortable before I walk away from any project. No tech background required.
How quickly can you respond when something breaks?▾
Monthly plan clients get priority response within 24 hours. For one-time clients I aim to respond within 48 hours. Urgent situations — call or text directly and I'll triage same-day when possible.
Can you build an online store for my existing business?▾
Absolutely. I can build on Shopify, Squarespace, or a custom platform depending on your needs and budget. We'll talk through your products, payment setup, and inventory management before picking a platform.
Do I own my website when you're done building it?▾
Yes, always. Your domain, your hosting account, your content — all in your name. I don't hold anything hostage. If you ever want to move to a different provider or developer, you can take everything with you.
Enterprise / Power Platform Questions
We have an inherited environment with no unmanaged source. Can you help?▾
Yes — this is a specialty. I've worked directly on environments with 180+ tables and locked managed solutions with no unmanaged source. The approach involves gap analysis tooling, solution coverage checks, and a structured segmentation strategy to rebuild a manageable ALM foundation without touching production.
What does a typical ALM engagement look like?▾
It starts with an environment audit — solution inventory, dependency mapping, pipeline review. From there we define a segmentation strategy (Table Schema → Columns → Views → Business Rules → Forms → Flows → Apps), build a repo structure and PAC CLI-based deployment scripts, establish a Git branching convention, and implement staged release workflows. Knowledge transfer and documentation are always included.
Do you work remotely or require on-site presence?▾
All Power Platform and ALM work is done remotely — it's fully cloud-native. I work across time zones and collaborate via your team's existing tools (Teams, DevOps, Slack, etc.). On-site is available for Missouri-based clients who want it, but it's rarely necessary.
Can you work alongside our internal development team?▾
Absolutely — that's often the best arrangement. I can serve as ALM architect and pipeline engineer while your team handles business logic development, or augment capacity on specific tracks. I document everything thoroughly so your team owns what we build together.
We're dealing with phantom connection references blocking our deployments.▾
Yes — including runtime-generated connection references from polymorphic lookup columns that don't appear in pac solution create-settings output. These require a catch-all deployment settings approach combined with stub connection references by logical name. I've solved this specific problem and can get your pipeline unblocked.
